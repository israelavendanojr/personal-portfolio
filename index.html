About Me

From Hobby to Passion

Hello! I’m Israel, a Computer Science student at Western Washington University, graduating in Spring 2026 with a minor in Mathematics.

My programming journey began during COVID, where I first explored game development through Unity and C#. Years later, my hobby has turned into a genuine passion for building meaningful tools and solving real-world problems.

Rooted in Community

As a first-generation student, I’ve found purpose in volunteering and participating in student-led organizations. These experiences have taught me the value of leadership, teamwork, and staying committed to causes beyond myself.

Above all, I aim to use my skills not just to build software—but to create lasting, positive impact, especially for underserved communities and causes that matter.

Balance & Growth

Outside of academics, I’m passionate about fitness, cooking, and family. Fitness keeps me goal-oriented, cooking helps me explore new things, and my family keeps me grounded and inspired. These interests shape how I work—with structure, curiosity, and a growth mindset.

I’m currently seeking Summer 2025 internships where I can apply my experience, grow as a developer, and contribute to meaningful work. If my values resonate with your mission, I’d love to connect!
